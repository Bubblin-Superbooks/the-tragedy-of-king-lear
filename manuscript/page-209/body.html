Scene Vii.

A Tent In The French Camp

Enter Cordelia, Kent, Doctor, and Gentleman

Cor. O thou good Kent, how shall I live and workTo match thy goodness? My life will be too shortAnd every measure fail me

Kent. To be acknowledg'd, madam, is o'erpaid.All my reports go with the modest truth;Nor more nor clipp'd, but so

Cor. Be better suited.These weeds are memories of those worser hours.I prithee put them off

Kent. Pardon, dear madam.Yet to be known shortens
