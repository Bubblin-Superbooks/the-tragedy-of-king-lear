Kent. Made she no verbal question?
Gent. Faith, once or twice she heav'd the name of fatherPantingly forth, as if it press'd her heart;Cried 'Sisters, sisters! Shame of ladies! Sisters!Kent! father! sisters! What, i' th' storm? i' th' night?Let pity not be believ'd!' There she shookThe holy water from her heavenly eyes,And clamour moisten'd. Then away she startedTo deal with grief alone

Kent. It is the stars,The stars above us, govern our conditions;Else one self mate and mate could not begetSuch different issues. You spoke not with her since?
Gent. No

Kent. Was this before the King return'd?
