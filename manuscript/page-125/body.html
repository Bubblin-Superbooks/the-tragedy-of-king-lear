Scene Ii.

Another Part Of The Heath

Storm still. Enter Lear and Fool

Lear. Blow, winds, and crack your cheeks! rage! blow!You cataracts and hurricanoes, spoutTill you have drench'd our steeples, drown'd the cocks!You sulph'rous and thought-executing fires,Vaunt-couriers to oak-cleaving thunderbolts,Singe my white head! And thou, all-shaking thunder,Strike flat the thick rotundity o' th' world,Crack Nature's moulds, all germains spill at once,That makes ingrateful man! Fool. O nuncle, court
