Glou. Let go my hand.Here, friend, is another purse; in it a jewelWell worth a poor man's taking. Fairies and godsProsper it with thee! Go thou further off;Bid me farewell, and let me hear thee going

Edg. Now fare ye well, good sir

Glou. With all my heart

Edg. [aside]. Why I do trifle thus with his despairIs done to cure it

Glou. O you mighty gods! He kneels.This world I do renounce, and, in your sights,Shake patiently my great affliction off.If I could bear it longer and not fallTo quarrel with your great opposeless wills,My snuff and loathed part of nature shouldBurn itself out. If Edgar live, O, bless him!Now, fellow, fare thee well. He falls [forward and swoons]
