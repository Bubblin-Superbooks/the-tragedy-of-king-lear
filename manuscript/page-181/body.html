Kent. Who hath he left behind him general?
Gent. The Marshal of France, Monsieur La Far

Kent. Did your letters pierce the Queen to any demonstration ofgrief?
Gent. Ay, sir. She took them, read them in my presence,And now and then an ample tear trill'd downHer delicate cheek. It seem'd she was a queenOver her passion, who, most rebel-like,Sought to be king o'er her

Kent. O, then it mov'd her?
Gent. Not to a rage. Patience and sorrow stroveWho should express her goodliest. You have seenSunshine and rain at once: her smiles and tearsWere like, a better way. Those happy smiletsThat play'd on her ripe lip seem'd not to knowWhat guests were in her eyes, which parted thenceAs pearls from diamonds dropp'd. In brief,Sorrow would be a rarity most belov'd,If all could so become it
