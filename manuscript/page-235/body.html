Alb. Which is that adversary?
Edg. What's he that speaks for Edmund Earl of Gloucester?
Edm. Himself. What say'st thou to him?
Edg. Draw thy sword,That, if my speech offend a noble heart,Thy arm may do thee justice. Here is mine.Behold, it is the privilege of mine honours,My oath, and my profession. I protest-Maugre thy strength, youth, place, and eminence,Despite thy victor sword and fire-new fortune,Thy valour and thy heart- thou art a traitor;False to thy gods, thy brother, and thy father;Conspirant 'gainst this high illustrious prince;And from th' extremest upward of thy headTo the descent and dust beneath thy foot,A most toad-spotted traitor. Say thou 'no,'This sword, this arm, and my best spirits are bentTo prove upon thy heart, whereto I speak,Thou liest
